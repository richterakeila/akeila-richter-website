Building at the edge of
health & human decision-making

Founder & CTO, Huddle

The data exists. Lab results, clinical notes, wearable metrics, and more health info are captured more than ever. Yet many people, especially millennials navigating significant life choices, can't access or interpret this data to impact their lives. The gap is not about content. It is an understanding and interpretation problem, costing people their agency over their health. I am building the bridge.

⬡
The Problem I Live In

There is a systemic gap in the flow of health information from clinical systems to the public, alongside rising rates of obesity and metabolic diseases. Many people do not know enough about key health factors like how their hormones influence their daily energy, mood, focus, and nutritional choices. We need to address this knowledge gap for better individual health and well-being.

◈
The Gap I'm Closing

Electronic health records and consumer wellness tools exist in separate worlds. One holds the data; the other holds the attention. Neither speaks the language of the person in the middle, trying to make better decisions with incomplete information. That person deserves both.

◎
Why I'm the One to Build It

Twenty years engineering complex systems, a foundation in Human Psychology, and a career spent making critical information accessible to decision-makers at the highest levels. Now applied to the most personal health decisions people face every day.

Born in Chicago and raised across the globe thanks to parents in military service, Akeila S. Richter is fluent in navigating complex systems, whether geopolitical, organizational, or technological.

She holds a Bachelor of Science in Industrial Engineering and a Master of Engineering in Industrial & Information Systems Engineering from Morgan State University, complemented by deep studies in Human Psychology. The rare pairing of rigorous systems thinking with genuine human insight defines everything Akeila builds and leads.

Across every role she has held as an engineer, technical writer, organizer, and events entrepreneur, the through line has been the same. She sees the gap that others overlook and steps in to help build what should be there. That is a founder's instinct, and it has been present across every chapter of her career.

Today, Akeila is building her next chapter from Washington, DC, where she lives with her husband and daughter.
